## Top New Features in Copilot for Windows

### 1. Edit Documents by Default with Copilot in Word

**What it is:** 
Copilot in Word is now more powerful and intuitive than ever. The default Copilot chat experience in Word now allows Copilot to directly edit your documents. You no longer need to switch modes or tools—Copilot is ready to make changes right when you ask. All changes made by Copilot are fully reviewable and reversible, giving you complete control over the final output [1].

**Why it's useful:** 
This feature drastically reduces the friction of getting started. You can open a blank document, prompt Copilot, and it will automatically turn on "Edit with Copilot," keeping you in a continuous, AI-assisted flow where you can iterate quickly without breaking your concentration.

**How to use it:**
1. Open Microsoft Word and create a new blank document or open an existing one.
2. Look for the Copilot icon or prompt box in the document.
3. Type a prompt, such as "Draft a project proposal for a new marketing campaign."
4. Copilot will generate the text and directly edit the document.
5. Review the changes. You can accept, reject, or ask Copilot to refine specific sections.

### 2. Copilot is Agentic in PowerPoint

**What it is:** 
Copilot is now "agentic" in PowerPoint, meaning it can act as your personal presentation assistant. You can create, edit, and refine presentations through natural conversation directly within the app. Copilot can generate slides, update content, improve layouts, and polish designs while preserving your formatting, structure, and branding [1].

**Why it's useful:** 
Creating polished presentations often requires tedious formatting and layout adjustments. With this feature, you can simply tell Copilot what you want—like "shorten this presentation to five slides" or "make the tone more professional"—and it will handle the heavy lifting. It can even pull context from your files, meetings, and emails to shape the content.

**How to use it:**
1. Open PowerPoint and start a new presentation or open an existing one.
2. Click on the Copilot icon to open the chat pane.
3. Type a request, such as "Create a 5-slide presentation based on my recent marketing plan document."
4. Once the slides are generated, you can ask Copilot to make specific edits, like "Change the layout of slide 3 to include a comparison table."
5. Review the updated slides and finalize your presentation.

## Cool Tip or Trick

### Search for Meetings by Organizer using Copilot Chat

**What it is:** 
Did you know you can use Copilot Chat to quickly search your calendar for meetings organized by a specific person? This is a fantastic, lesser-known feature that can save you from endlessly scrolling through your Outlook calendar [2].

**Why it's useful:** 
If you need to prepare for an upcoming 1-on-1 or review action items from past meetings with a specific colleague or manager, this trick helps you locate those calendar items and prioritize your activities in seconds.

**How to use it:**
1. Open Copilot Chat on your Windows PC.
2. Type a prompt specifically asking about an organizer. For example: "Find meetings organized by Alex Johnson next week."
3. Alternatively, you can ask for summaries: "Summarize action items from the last 3 meetings organized by Sarah Davis."
4. Copilot will instantly retrieve the relevant calendar events and provide the information you requested.

## Now on Mobile

### Copilot Mobile Widgets and Action Button

**What it is:** 
Microsoft has introduced new Copilot mobile widgets for both iOS and Android, along with a dedicated action button for iOS. These widgets bring Microsoft 365 Copilot features right to your mobile device’s Home and Lock screens [1].

**Why it's useful:** 
When you're on the go, you don't always have time to open an app and navigate through menus. With just one tap from your home screen, you can start a chat, activate a voice conversation, or open your camera to attach a photo to Copilot Chat.

**How to use it (iOS & Android):**
1. **iOS:** Long-press on your Home Screen or Lock Screen, tap the "+" icon, search for "Copilot," and add the widget of your choice. You can also configure the Action Button (on supported iPhone models) to launch Copilot.
2. **Android:** Long-press on an empty space on your Home Screen, select "Widgets," scroll down to "Copilot," and drag your preferred widget onto the screen.
3. Tap the widget to instantly start a text chat, voice conversation, or visual search with Copilot.
