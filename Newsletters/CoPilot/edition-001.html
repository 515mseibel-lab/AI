Microsoft 365 Copilot Update

The latest enterprise Copilot features for your M365 work apps — made simple for your team
Edition 001
📅 March 17, 2026

Hey DCN team! 👋 Welcome to the very first edition of DCN Tech Corner — your monthly guide to the newest Microsoft 365 Copilot features built right into the apps you use every day: Outlook, Teams, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Copilot Chat. We keep it short, practical, and jargon-free so you can start using these features right away. Let’s dive in! 🏃

✅  All features in this newsletter are officially confirmed by Microsoft and apply to Microsoft 365 Copilot (enterprise/work version) — no rumors, no guesswork.
📌 What is Microsoft 365 Copilot? It’s the AI assistant built into your work apps — Outlook, Teams, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Copilot Chat. If your organization has a Microsoft 365 Copilot license, everything in this newsletter is available to you right now!

💬 New in Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat

📢 Copilot Chat Feature 1 of 2

Highlight Any Text & Ask Copilot About It!

💡 What’s New? You can now select any text in a Copilot response and instantly ask a follow-up question about just that part. No more getting a broad answer when you only need to dig into one specific sentence — just highlight and ask!
🌟 Why You’ll Love It Imagine Copilot gives you a long summary. You spot one sentence about a policy change and want more detail. Instead of retyping your whole question, just highlight that sentence and click “Ask Copilot.” Instant deep-dive, zero extra effort.
Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat — Highlight text, Ask Copilot button appears
Select any text in a Copilot response and the "Ask Copilot" button appears instantly
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat — go to Microsoft365.com, click the Copilot icon in the left sidebar, or open it from Teams.
  2. 2Ask Copilot any question and wait for the response.
  3. 3Use your mouse to click and drag to highlight any part of the response text.
  4. 4A small “Ask Copilot” button will pop up above your selection — click it!
  5. 5Type your follow-up question about that specific text and get a focused, precise answer.
💬 Try This After Highlighting
"Can you explain this in simpler terms?"

⏰ Rolling out March 2026 — Requires Microsoft 365 Copilot license — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

📢 Copilot Chat Feature 2 of 2

Ground Copilot on Your SharePoint Sites & Lists!

💡 What’s New? You can now type a forward slash “/” in the Copilot Chat message box to pull in a specific SharePoint site, list, or file as the source for your question. Copilot will answer using only that content — no guessing, no mixing in unrelated info.
🌟 Why You’ll Love It Perfect for asking questions about your specific team’s policies, procedures, or documents stored in SharePoint. Type /, pick your site, and ask away. Copilot stays laser-focused on exactly what you need.
Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat — Type "/" to see SharePoint Sites picker
Type "/" in the Copilot Chat box and select "Sites" to ground Copilot on your SharePoint content
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat (Microsoft365.com or Teams) and make sure you are in Work mode — this is the mode that accesses your organization’s data.
  2. 2Click in the message box and type a forward slash: /
  3. 3A picker will appear — click the “Sites” tab to browse your SharePoint sites.
  4. 4Click the site or list you want Copilot to use as its source.
  5. 5Type your question — Copilot will answer using only that site’s content!
💬 Example Prompt After Selecting a Site
"What is our policy on patient appointment cancellations?"

⏰ Rolling out March 2026 — Requires Microsoft 365 Copilot license — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

📢 Also New in Microsoft 365 This Month

📅 New in Outlook — Schedule with Copilot

Let Copilot Schedule Your Meetings Automatically!

💡 What’s New? A new “Schedule with Copilot” button now appears at the top of email threads in the new Outlook. Click it and Copilot checks everyone’s calendar, suggests the best times, drafts the agenda, and sends the invite — all without leaving your inbox!
🌟 Why You’ll Love It No more back-and-forth emails trying to find a time that works. Copilot does all the calendar detective work for you in seconds.
Microsoft Outlook — Schedule with Copilot button at top of email thread
The "Schedule with Copilot" button appears at the top of any email thread in the new Outlook
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Microsoft Outlook (the new Outlook for Windows — part of your Microsoft 365 subscription) and click on any email thread.
  2. 2Look for the “Schedule with Copilot” button at the top of the email — click it.
  3. 3Copilot will open a guided chat and check all attendees’ calendars automatically.
  4. 4Review the suggested time slots — Copilot shows who is available for each option.
  5. 5Confirm your choice and Copilot will draft the agenda, book the room, and send the invite — done!

✅ Rolled out February 2026 — Requires Microsoft 365 Copilot license & new Outlook — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

💡 Cool Microsoft 365 Copilot Tip of the Month

📊 Ask Copilot How Much Time You Spend in Meetings!

Get a Visual Breakdown of Your Meeting Time

💡 The Trick Did you know you can ask Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat to analyze your calendar and show you exactly how much time you’ve spent in meetings? It even generates a bar chart broken down by week! This is a hidden gem that most people don’t know about.
🌟 Why You’ll Love It It’s a great way to reflect on your schedule, spot meeting-heavy weeks, and make a case for more focused work time. Takes 5 seconds and gives you real data about your own calendar.
Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat — Meeting time bar chart response
Copilot generates a visual bar chart showing your meeting hours per week when you ask
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat — go to Microsoft365.com or open it in Teams.
  2. 2Make sure you are in Work mode (this gives Copilot access to your calendar data).
  3. 3Type the prompt below and press Enter.
  4. 4Copilot will return a bar chart showing your meeting hours per week, plus bullet-point insights.
  5. 5Follow up with “Which meetings could I decline?” for bonus productivity advice!
💬 Copy This Prompt
"How much time did I spend in meetings last month? Show me a chart broken down by week."

✅ Available now — Requires Microsoft 365 Copilot license — Source: Microsoft Learn Release Notes

📱 Microsoft 365 Copilot — Now on Mobile

📱 New: Microsoft 365 Copilot Home Screen Widgets!

Put Your Work AI Assistant Right on Your Home Screen

💡 What’s New? Microsoft has launched new Microsoft 365 Copilot home screen and lock screen widgets for both iPhone and Android! These widgets connect directly to your work account — so you get your work AI assistant right on your home screen. iPhone users on supported models can also set their Action Button to launch Copilot instantly.
🌟 Why You’ll Love It One tap from your home screen and you’re instantly chatting with your work AI. No unlocking apps, no navigating menus — just tap and ask.
Microsoft 365 Copilot widget on iOS and Android home screens
The M365 Copilot widget shows the colorful ribbon icon with New Chat and Voice quick actions
 iOS Setup
🤖 Android Setup
📋 iOS Setup — Step by Step
  1. 1Make sure you have the Microsoft 365 app installed on your iPhone (free from the App Store).
  2. 2Long-press on an empty space on your home screen until icons start wiggling.
  3. 3Tap the + button in the top-left corner to open the widget gallery.
  4. 4Search for “Microsoft 365” or “Copilot” in the search bar.
  5. 5Select the Copilot widget and tap “Add Widget” — done!
📋 Android Setup — Step by Step
  1. 1Make sure you have the Microsoft 365 app installed on your Android phone.
  2. 2Long-press on an empty space on your Android home screen.
  3. 3Tap “Widgets” from the menu that appears.
  4. 4Scroll to find “Copilot” in the widget list.
  5. 5Long-press the widget and drag it to your preferred spot on the home screen.
