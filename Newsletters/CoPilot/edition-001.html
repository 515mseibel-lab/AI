💬 New in Copilot Chat

🆕 Copilot Chat Feature 1 of 2

Highlight Any Text & Ask Copilot About It!

💡 What's New? You can now select any text in a Copilot response and instantly ask a follow-up question about just that part. No more getting a broad answer when you only need to dig into one specific sentence — just highlight and ask!
🌟 Why You'll Love It Imagine Copilot gives you a long summary. You spot one sentence about a policy change and want more detail. Instead of retyping your whole question, just highlight that sentence and click "Ask Copilot." Instant deep-dive, zero extra effort.
Copilot Chat — Highlight text and Ask Copilot button appears
Select any text in a Copilot response and the "Ask Copilot" button appears instantly
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Copilot Chat on your Windows PC (copilot.microsoft.com or via the taskbar icon).
  2. 2Ask Copilot any question and wait for the response.
  3. 3Use your mouse to click and drag to highlight any part of the response text.
  4. 4A small "Ask Copilot" button will pop up above your selection — click it!
  5. 5Type your follow-up question about that specific text and get a focused, precise answer.
💬 Try This After Highlighting
"Can you explain this in simpler terms?"

⏰ Rolling out in March 2026 — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

🆕 Copilot Chat Feature 2 of 2

Ground Copilot on Your SharePoint Sites & Lists!

💡 What's New? You can now type a forward slash "/" in the Copilot Chat message box to pull in a specific SharePoint site or list as the context for your question. Copilot will answer based on your organization's actual data — not just general knowledge.
🌟 Why You'll Love It Need to ask about a specific policy document, patient resource guide, or staff handbook? Just type "/" and pick the right SharePoint site. Copilot will give you answers grounded in that exact content — no more guessing if the info is current or relevant.
Copilot Chat — Type "/" to see SharePoint Sites picker
Type "/" in the Copilot Chat box to browse and select your SharePoint sites
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Copilot Chat and make sure you are in Work mode (toggle at the top).
  2. 2Click in the message box and type a forward slash: /
  3. 3A picker will appear — click the "Sites" tab to browse your SharePoint sites.
  4. 4Click the site or list you want Copilot to use as its source.
  5. 5Type your question — Copilot will answer using only that site's content!
Copilot Chat — Sites picker showing organization SharePoint sites
The Sites tab shows all your available SharePoint sites to ground your Copilot conversation
💬 Example Prompt After Selecting a Site
"What is our policy on patient appointment cancellations?"

⏰ Rolling out in March 2026 — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

🆕 Also New This Month

🗓️ New Feature

Schedule Meetings Directly from Any Email!

💡 What's New? You can now click "Schedule with Copilot" directly from any email thread in Outlook. Copilot will automatically check everyone's calendars, find available times, book a meeting room, draft an agenda, and send the invite — all in one guided flow.
🌟 Why You'll Love It No more back-and-forth emails trying to find a time that works for everyone. Spot an email that needs a follow-up meeting? One click and Copilot handles the whole scheduling process for you — from finding a time to sending the invite.
Microsoft Outlook — Schedule with Copilot button and suggested meeting times
Click "Schedule with Copilot" from any email and Copilot finds the best times for everyone
📋 How To Use It — Step by Step
  1. 1Open Microsoft Outlook (new Outlook) and click on any email thread.
  2. 2Look for the "Schedule with Copilot" button at the top of the email — click it.
  3. 3Copilot will open a guided chat and check all attendees' calendars automatically.
  4. 4Review the suggested time slots — Copilot shows who is available for each option.
  5. 5Confirm your choice and Copilot will draft the agenda, book the room, and send the invite — done!

✅ Rolled out February 2026 (new Outlook) — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

💡 Cool Tip of the Month

⚡ Pro Tip — Works in Copilot Chat

Ask Copilot How Much Time You Spend in Meetings!

💡 The Trick Did you know you can ask Copilot to analyze your meeting habits and show you exactly how much time you spend in meetings — broken down by category and compared month over month? It even generates charts! No extra tools needed.
🌟 Why You'll Love It Feeling like you're always in meetings? Now you can actually prove it — or disprove it! Use this to make smarter decisions about your schedule, identify which meeting types are eating your time, and have data-backed conversations about workload.
Copilot Chat — Meeting time analytics with bar chart
Copilot generates a visual chart showing your meeting hours by month and category
📋 Try These Prompts Right Now
  1. 1Open Copilot Chat on your Windows PC.
  2. 2Make sure you are in Work mode.
  3. 3Type one of these prompts and hit Enter:
💬 Prompt 1 — Basic
"How much time did I spend in meetings last month?"
💬 Prompt 2 — Detailed Breakdown
"How much time did I spend in meetings last month, split by category — and how does that compare to the previous month?"
💬 Prompt 3 — Visual Chart
"Create a month-by-month bar chart comparing my meeting time for the last 3 months."

✅ Rolled out February 2026 — Source: Microsoft 365 Copilot Blog, Feb 2026

📱 Now on Mobile

📱 iOS & Android

Copilot is Now on Your Home Screen!

💡 What's New Microsoft has launched new Copilot home screen and lock screen widgets for both iPhone and Android! iPhone users on supported models can also set their Action Button to launch Copilot instantly. One tap and you're chatting with AI — no unlocking, no searching for the app!
🍎 iPhone (iOS)
🤖 Android
Mobile — Copilot Home Screen Widget on iPhone and Android
Add the Copilot widget to your home screen for instant one-tap access
📋 iPhone Setup — Step by Step
  1. 1Long-press on an empty area of your Home Screen until the icons start to jiggle.
  2. 2Tap the "+" button in the top-left corner to open the widget picker.
  3. 3Search for "Copilot" in the widget search bar.
  4. 4Select your preferred widget size and tap "Add Widget."
  5. 5Bonus: Go to Settings > Action Button (on supported iPhone models) and assign it to open Copilot.
Android — Adding the Copilot Widget to Home Screen
Android users can add the Copilot widget from the widget picker
📋 Android Setup — Step by Step
  1. 1Long-press on an empty space on your Android home screen.
  2. 2Tap "Widgets" from the menu that appears.
  3. 3Scroll to find "Copilot" in the widget list.
  4. 4Long-press the widget and drag it to your preferred spot on the home screen.
  5. 5Tap the widget to instantly start a chat, voice conversation, or open the camera with Copilot!
