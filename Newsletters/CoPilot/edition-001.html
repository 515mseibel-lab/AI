🌟 Top New Features in Copilot for Windows

Feature 1 of 2

Edit Documents by Default with Copilot in Word

What It Is

Copilot in Word can now directly edit your documents through the chat pane. You no longer need to switch modes or tools — Copilot is ready to make changes the moment you ask. Every change is fully reviewable and reversible, so you stay in control.

Why It's Useful

Opening a blank page is often the hardest part. Now, just open Word, type a prompt, and Copilot will start writing and editing for you automatically. You can keep iterating in a continuous AI-assisted flow without breaking your concentration.

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Open Microsoft Word and create a new blank document or open an existing one.
  2. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon or look for the prompt box in the document.
  3. Type a prompt, for example: "Draft a project proposal for a new marketing campaign."
  4. Copilot will generate the text and directly insert it into your document.
  5. Review the changes. You can accept, reject, or refine specific sections by asking Copilot to adjust them.

Feature 2 of 2

Copilot is Now Agentic in PowerPoint

What It Is

Copilot is now your personal PowerPoint presentation assistant. You can create, edit, and refine entire presentations through natural conversation. Copilot generates slides, updates content, improves layouts, and polishes designs — all while preserving your formatting, structure, and branding.

Why It's Useful

No more tedious formatting or layout adjustments. Just tell Copilot what you want — like "shorten this to five slides" or "make the tone more professional" — and it handles the rest. It can even pull context from your files, meetings, and emails to shape the content.

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Open PowerPoint and start a new presentation or open an existing one.
  2. Click the Copilot icon in the ribbon to open the chat pane.
  3. Type a request, such as: "Create a 5-slide presentation based on my recent marketing plan document."
  4. Once slides are generated, ask Copilot to make specific edits, like: "Change slide 3 to include a comparison table."
  5. Review the updated slides and finalize your presentation.

💡 Cool Tip or Trick

⚡ Pro Tip

Search for Meetings by Organizer Using Copilot Chat

What It Is

Did you know you can ask Copilot Chat to instantly find meetings on your calendar organized by a specific person? No more scrolling endlessly through Outlook — just ask Copilot!

Why It's Useful

Need to prepare for a 1-on-1 or review action items from past meetings with your manager or a colleague? This trick helps you locate those calendar items and prioritize your day in seconds.

Step-by-Step Instructions
  1. Open Copilot Chat on your Windows PC.
  2. Type a prompt like: "Find meetings organized by Alex Johnson next week."
  3. Or ask for summaries: "Summarize action items from the last 3 meetings organized by Sarah Davis."
  4. Copilot will instantly retrieve the relevant calendar events and provide the information you requested.

📱 Now on Mobile

📱 Mobile Feature

Copilot Home & Lock Screen Widgets

 iOS 🧩 Android
What It Is

Microsoft has introduced new Copilot widgets for both iOS and Android home and lock screens, plus a dedicated Action Button for supported iPhones. With just one tap, you can start a chat, activate a voice conversation, or open your camera to attach a photo to Copilot Chat — all without opening the app.

Why It's Useful

When you're on the go, you don't always have time to open an app and navigate through menus. The widget puts Copilot one tap away from anywhere on your phone.

iOS Instructions
  1. Long-press on your Home Screen or Lock Screen.
  2. Tap the "+" icon in the top-left corner.
  3. Search for "Copilot" and select your preferred widget size.
  4. Drag it onto your screen and tap Done.
  5. Bonus: On supported iPhone models, go to Settings > Action Button and assign it to launch Copilot.
Android Instructions
  1. Long-press on an empty space on your Home Screen.
  2. Select "Widgets" from the menu that appears.
  3. Scroll down to find "Copilot" in the widget list.
  4. Drag your preferred widget onto the home screen.
  5. Tap the widget to instantly start a chat, voice conversation, or visual search.
